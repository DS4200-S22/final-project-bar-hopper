Boston Bar Hopper

Zoheb Aziz, Ningyu Chen, Marco Tortolani, and Jason Zhang

Course Project as part of DS4200 S22: Information Visualization, taught by Prof. Ab Mosca, Data Visualization @ Khoury, Northeastern University.

Abstract

Our motivation behind the development of this tool is to simplify the exploration processes of all those looking to experience Boston's rich bar scene. We want you to spend time enjoying yourself at the perfect bar instead of poring endlessly through internet searches hoping to discover an acceptable establishment. To address these pain points, we have developed the Boston Bar Hopper, a custom visualization tool which leverages both classical and novel techniques to transform what was once a convoluted procedure into an enjoyable and seamless experience. This tool is suited for anyone looking to explore, discover, and identify Boston bar locations which most suit their preferences in pricing, rating, location, and much more.

Visualization Explanation

Our visualization consists of four distinct but interconnected views. The top two visualizations provide the high-level overview of the data while the bottom two allow for more targeted analysis of locations of interest.
The top left view is a map of Boston which allows you to quickly understand the geographical position of the bars, and it contains brushing and tooltip functionality to fulfill details-on-demand. In addition, individual bars can be selected by clicking on their points, which will display the location name as a hyperlink to its dedicated Yelp page, and also render the target location’s hours of operation in the time chart.
The top right view is a scatterplot of each location’s rating vs rating count, and it enables you to quickly identify highly rated and popular locations.
The bottom left view is a time chart which displays the selected location’s hours of operation. It also has a tooltip functionality to provide users with the most accurate information on scheduling.
The bottom right view is a relative geolocational positioning chart which displays the bars in their relative positions to a given location. When location services are enabled, it can automatically render with respect to your current location. You can set an upper limit on how far the rendered bars can be, and the graph will also identify the nearest bar.
